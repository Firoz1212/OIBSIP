Subhash chandra bose

The man also known as Netaji

Subhas Chandra Bose with Sarat Chandra Bose and Jawaharlal Nehru

Here's a time line of Subhash chandra bose:

  • 1897- was born on January 23, Cuttack, Orissa.
  • 1919-To fulfill his parents wishes he went to England to compete for Indian Civil Services.
  • 1920-In England he appeared for the Indian Civil Service competitive examination, and came out fourth in order of merit
  • 1921-he was deeply disturbed by the Jallianwalla Bagh massacre, and left his Civil Services apprenticeship midway to return to India
  • 1928-the Motilal Nehru Committee appointed by the Congress declared in favour of Domination Status, but Subhas Chandra Bose along with Jawaharlal Nehru opposed it, and both asserted that they would be satisfied with nothing short of complete independence for India.
  • 1930-he was jailed during Civil Disobedience movement.
  • 1931-He was released after Gandhi-Irwin pact was signed. He protested against the Gandhi-Irwin pact and opposed the suspension of Civil Disobedience movement specially when Bhagat Singh and his associates were hanged.Subash Chandra Bose was soon arrested again under the infamous Bengal Regulation.
  • 1937-After the General Elections, Congress came to power in seven states and Subash Chandra Bose was released
  • 1938- he was elected President of the Haripura Congress Session
  • 1941-Subhas Chandra Bose disappeared from his home in Calcutta and reached Germany via Afghanistan.
  • 1942-he began his regular broadcasts from Radio Berlin, which aroused tremendous enthusiasm in India.
  • 1943-he arrived in Singapore from Germany. In Singapore he took over the reins of the Indian Independence Movement in East Asia from Rash Behari Bose and organised the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) comprising mainly of Indian prisoners of war.
  • 1944-Azad Hind Fauj crossed the Burma Border, and stood on Indian soil on March 18.
  • 1945-was reportedly killed in an air crash over Taipeh, Taiwan (Formosa) on August 18. Though it is widely believed that he was still alive after the air crash not much information could be found about him.
